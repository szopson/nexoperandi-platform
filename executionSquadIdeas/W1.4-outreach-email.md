# W1.4 Brand Asset: Outreach (Email)

> Source: Execution Squad Mentoring - Week 1

---

## When to Choose Outreach

Choose outreach when you want fast signal from a clear list of buyers.

It is usually the cleanest start in B2B because you can reach decision makers directly and get replies fast.

Most people should start with outreach, validate the offer, then layer content or paid ads to scale.

But it is not absolute. Some lanes win faster with content. Some win faster with paid ads.

Inside Execution Squad, the job is always the same:

**Install the infrastructure. Track the numbers. Let the week decide.**

## Lead List Building

### Warm-First, Then Local-First

**Start warm-first.**

Anyone you already have a connection to, even a light one.

**If you cannot find 10 warm leads, go local-first.**

Same city, same boundary. Real businesses you can actually reach.

**If you cannot find 10 local leads, stop.**

Do not "fix the message."

Your first problem is lead reality.

### The 10-Lead Start (Required)

You only need 10 to start.

This is how you avoid hiding behind prep.

For each of the 10 leads, capture:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Name | Contact name |
| Company | Business name |
| Email | Direct email |
| Why them | One line reason |
| Segment | Perfect fit or good fit |

**If you cannot write "why them" in one line, they are not ready.**

### Expand After 10 (So Week 2 Can Run)

After you have 10, expand the list.

Target standard for a real week:

- 30+ reachable leads
- 10 "hot" leads you can reach within 48 hours

Hot leads are not "better companies."

**Hot means reachable now.**

### Where to Find Leads (Simple, Manual)

Use sources that give you real contact paths.

**Warm-first sources:**
- People you already messaged before
- Past clients, old coworkers, friends of friends
- Anyone who has already seen your name and will open

**Local-first sources:**
- Local business lists and directories
- Businesses with visible contact pages
- Businesses with booking links, inquiry forms, or active social pages

**Rule:** You are not trying to build a perfect database. You are building a list you can send today.

## Segment Rules (Perfect Fit vs Good Fit)

You are not choosing "better" or "worse."

You are choosing the right approach.

### Perfect Fit

**Perfect fit means you can record a Loom without guessing.**

You can point at something real.

Perfect fit triggers:
- You can name a specific observation in one line
- You can name one clear leak in one line
- You can record a Loom in under 5 minutes

### Good Fit

**Good fit means they look like a match, but you lack context.**

If you tried to Loom, you would be making things up.

Good fit triggers:
- You only have a broad guess, not a clear observation
- You need permission or one question answered first
- You want to avoid sending a fake Loom

## Daily Execution Plan (What You Actually Do)

Every day you do two moves:

1. Send 3 to 5 personal Looms to perfect fit leads
2. Fill the rest with good fit volume using the same spine

Perfect fit is precision.

Good fit is reps.

**Reply handling is daily. Same day.**

### Weekly Standard

| Type | Volume |
|------|--------|
| Perfect fit | 3-5 per day |
| Good fit volume | 30+ per day (weekdays) |

Do not "blast" a giant list in one day.

**Controlled volume only until the numbers prove it.**

---

## Message Spine (Use Everywhere)

Every email and Loom follows the same spine:

1. Observation
2. Leak
3. Cost
4. Fix
5. CTA (decision call)

If you remove parts, it gets soft.

If you add parts, it gets long and unclear.

## Pattern Break (Pick One and Lock It)

Pick one line. Use it all week. Do not rotate.

- "Quick one. I'll keep this short."
- "This is not a pitch email."
- "If this isn't relevant, reply 'no' and I'll close the loop."
- "I might be off. If I am, tell me and I'll disappear."

## Perfect Fit (Loom First)

### What You Do

1. Pick the lead
2. Record a 60-second Loom (face-only)
3. Send the email with the Loom link
4. Follow up twice
5. Route to the decision call
6. Log it

Perfect fit is not about being fancy.

**It is about being specific.**

### Perfect Fit Subject Lines (Pick One Set and Lock It)

- "Quick question about your follow-up"
- "Short video for you"
- "I noticed one leak"
- "Close the loop?"
- "Wrong person?"

### Perfect Fit Email 1 (Template)

```
Subject: [Locked subject]

Hi [First name],

Quick one.

[Pattern break line]

I noticed [Observation].

Usually the leak is [Leak].

When that happens, [Cost].

I recorded a short video so you can see what I mean:

[Loom link]

If this is worth fixing, book a decision call here:

[Decision call link]

If not, reply "no" and I'll close the loop.

[Your name]
```

### Perfect Fit Follow-Up 1 (Template)

```
Subject: Re: [Same subject]

Hi [First name],

Quick nudge.

Still relevant, or should I close the loop?

[Your name]
```

### Perfect Fit Follow-Up 2 (Template)

```
Subject: Close the loop?

Hi [First name],

Last note from me.

If this is not a priority, reply "no" and I'll close the loop.

[Your name]
```

## Good Fit (Permission First)

### What You Do

1. Send a permission email
2. If they reply yes, record the Loom
3. Send the Loom email
4. Follow up twice
5. Route to the decision call
6. Log it

Good fit prevents you from guessing.

**It keeps your credibility clean.**

### Good Fit Permission Email (Template)

```
Subject: Quick question

Hi [First name],

Quick one.

I think there might be a leak in how [Lead type] handles [Specific step].

I can record a short video to show you what I'm seeing.

Should I send it?

[Your name]
```

### Good Fit Loom Send Email (Template)

```
[Loom link]

If this is worth fixing, book a decision call here:

[Decision call link]

[Your name]
```

### Good Fit Follow-Up 2 (Template)

```

[Your name]
```

## Loom Script (60 Seconds, Face-Only)

**Goal:** Make them say "Yeah, that's us," then give one next step.

No teaching. No tool tour. No long story.

### Script

> "Hey [Name], quick video. I'll keep this simple.
>
> I noticed [Observation].
>
> Usually the leak is [Leak].
>
> When that happens, [Cost].
>
> The fix is [System] so [Next step] happens cleanly.
>
> If you want, book a decision call. If not, no worries."

### Loom CTA Button Text (Pick One and Lock It)

- "Book a decision call"
- "See if this is a fit"
- "Fix the leak"

---

## Reply Handling (Keep It Clean)

Every reply goes into one bucket:

- Interested
- Not now
- No

Your job in email is routing, not consulting.

**If they want details, they get the decision call.**

### Interested (Template)

```
Perfect. Here's the decision call link:

[Decision call link]

Here's the one-page overview too:

[Offer doc link]
```

### Not Now (Template)

```
Got it.

Is this a next week thing or a next month thing?
```

Lock a follow-up date. **Do not leave it open.**

### No (Template)

```
Got it.

I'll close the loop.
```

## What to Log (Minimum Fields)

Log reality, not memory.

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Lead name | Contact name |
| Company | Business name |
| Segment | Perfect fit / good fit |
| Email | Contact email |
| Status | sent / follow-up / replied / booked / show / closed |
| Loom link | If used |
| Reply type | interested / not now / no |
| Next action | What happens next |
| Notes | One line |

## Scoreboard Tie-In (Track the Right Numbers)

Outreach execution definitions:

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| Execution | Outbound touches sent today |
| Positive signal | Replies with real engagement (not "wrong person") |
| Booked | Decision calls booked today |
| Show | Calls that happened today |
| Close | Deals committed today |
| Cash collected | Money actually collected today |

**Update daily. No backfilling on day 7.**

# Decision Call Flow (20-25 Minutes)

> Source: Execution Squad Mentoring

---

## 0) Pre-Call Setup (30 Seconds)

Have these open:
- The one-page offer doc
- The scoreboard
- Your calendar
- The next step you want (Start date or install date)

---

## 1) Opening (60 Seconds)

> "Quick note before we start.
>
> This is a decision call.
>
> In the next 20 minutes, we'll see if this is a fit.
>
> If it is, we'll pick a start date.
>
> If it's not, I'll tell you that too. Sound good?"

**If they hesitate:**

> "No pressure. I just don't do 'maybe' calls. If you only want info, I can send the one-page doc and we can stop here."

---

## 2) Context (2-3 Minutes)

**Goal:** Understand the current flow in plain words.

Ask:
- "What do you sell?"
- "How do leads come in today?"
- "What happens after someone inquires?"
- "Where do you think it breaks?"
- "What happens when it breaks?"

**Listen for the leak. Keep them talking.**

---

## 3) Pain And Cost (2-3 Minutes)

**Goal:** Make the leak real.

Ask:
- "How often does that happen?"
- "What does it cost you when it happens?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "Why didn't that stick?"

**If they dodge:**

> "Give me a rough answer. I'm not auditing you. I just need the shape."

---

## 4) Target Outcome (2 Minutes)

**Goal:** Define one measurable outcome.

Ask:
- "If we fix one thing next, what do you want to be true?"
- "What does 'working' look like?"
- "What number matters most right now?" (Booked, Show, Close, Cash)

**If they list five goals:**

> "Pick the top one. We cannot fix five things at once."

---

## 5) Fit Filter (2 Minutes)

**Goal:** Decide fast if you can win.

Ask:
- "Do you already have demand, or are we creating demand from zero?"
- "Can someone respond to leads the same day?"
- "Can you give access and approvals fast this week?"
- "Once we start, can you keep the offer and CTA locked for 7 days?"

**If a key answer kills it, end clean. Do not drag it.**

---

## 6) Simple Diagnosis (60 Seconds)

Say what you heard in one sentence.

> "Here's what I'm hearing.
>
> You have [Demand source]. The leak is [Leak].
>
> That's why you're losing [Cost]."

Then ask:

> "Is that accurate?"

---

## 7) The Offer In One Sentence (30 Seconds)

**Do not ramble.**

> "The fix is simple.
>
> We install [System] so [Next step] happens consistently, and we track it with a scoreboard so we stop guessing."

**Then stop.**

---

## 8) Scope And Timeline (2 Minutes)

Keep it clean. This prevents custom chaos.

> "What's included is:
> - [Deliverable 1]
> - [Deliverable 2]
> - [Deliverable 3]
> - [Deliverable 4]
>
> What's not included is:
> - [Boundary 1]
> - [Boundary 2]
> - [Boundary 3]"

**Timeline:**

> "We can start [Day]. The install runs [Time]."

---

## 9) Price (15 Seconds)

Say it once.

> "It's [Price]."

**Then stay quiet.**

---

## 10) Close To A Decision (60 Seconds)

> "Do you want to move forward?"

**If yes:**

> "Good. Two steps.
>
> First, pick a start date.
>
> Second, I'll send the invoice and the access checklist."

**If not now:**

> "All good. What's the real blocker?"
>
> "Is it timing, money, or trust?"

Then answer only that part. Short. Return to the decision.

**If no:**

> "Got it. Not a fit. Appreciate the time."

---

## Objection Handling (Only The Ones We Allow)

### "Send Me More Info"

> "I can. But info doesn't fix this.
>
> The one-page doc is the info.
>
> The real question is: Do you want to fix it?"

### "I Need To Think"

> "Totally. What part?"

Then force the category:
- Timing
- Money
- Trust

Answer only that part. Short. Return to the decision.

### "I Need To Talk To My Partner"

> "Cool. What do they need to decide?"

Set a follow-up time on the call. **Do not leave it open-ended.**

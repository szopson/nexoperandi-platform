# W1.5 Brand Asset: Content (Long Form + Short Form + Text)

> Source: Execution Squad Mentoring - Week 1

---

## When to Choose Content

Choose content when you want compounding reach and inbound over time.

It becomes the scale layer after your offer has signal.

Most people validate with outreach first, then move into content and go all-in.

But it is not absolute. If you already have content skill, do not throw it away just to start from outreach.

Inside Execution Squad, the job stays the same either way.

**Install the infrastructure. Track the numbers. Let the week decide.**

---

## What This Document Is

This is a content kit you can run immediately.

It is long on purpose so nothing is missing.

The content game is simple:

1. Research winners (5 to 7)
2. Change the angle into your offer language
3. Write the script or post
4. Produce (shoot or write)
5. Publish
6. Handle replies daily
7. Log signal
8. Fix one thing next week

**This is version 1. A hypothesis.**

No judging. No rewriting daily.

Run it, then change it based on results.

---

## Lock Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Lock these for 7 days once you start running:

- One primary content type (Long form, short form, or text)
- One offer doc link
- One CTA link (Decision call, one CTA only)
- One core message you repeat
- One posting rhythm you can actually keep

Write improvements in notes.

**Do not change the core mid-week.**

Changes happen on Decision Day only.

---

## What Counts as Signal

**Signal is not views. Signal is intent.**

### Count as signal:
- Comments that ask real questions
- DMs that ask "How?" or "Can you help?"
- Replies that push back on your point
- People who click your link
- People who ask for the next step
- Booked decision calls

### Ignore vanity:
- Generic likes
- "Great post" comments
- Random follows with no intent

---

## The Message Spine (Use Everywhere)

Every piece follows the same spine:

1. Hook
2. Problem
3. Solution
4. CTA

Keep it clean.

If you add extra layers, it turns into noise.

**CTA stays one line. One link. One next step.**

## Long Form Kit (YouTube)

### When to Choose Long Form

Choose long form when trust is the bottleneck and you can speak clearly on camera.

This is the best "main engine" when you want your audience to understand the system and take the next step.

### Output Rules

- Long form: One per week
- One CTA only
- One core message repeated for the week
- Short form can support long form, but the long form is the anchor

### Workflow

**Step 1: Research winners (5 to 7)**

Pick 5 to 7 winning long-form videos in your lane.

Do not pick what you personally like. Pick what clearly got attention and real engagement.

Save these from each winner:
- The hook
- The main point
- The flow (how it moves)
- The CTA style
- The comments (what people asked, what they argued about)

**Step 2: Angle change (Turn it into your offer language)**

Keep the structure. Change the language.

Swap these parts:
- Who it is for
- The leak
- The cost
- The fix
- The CTA

Angle change checklist:
- Does the hook match our voice?
- Does the problem describe a loop people live in?
- Does the solution stay simple and executable?
- Is the CTA one line and one link only?
- Did I remove tool talk as the lead?

**Step 3: Script writing (Full script)**

Write a script you can read out loud.

Do not write like a blog post. Write like you talk.

Keep it tight:
- Hook: One clear punch
- Problem: Show the loop, not definitions
- Solution: Actions, not theory
- CTA: One line, one link

**Step 4: Demo capture (Separate from talking head)**

Demos are not "features." They are proof the system is real.

Keep demos simple:
- Show the flow end-to-end
- Show the next step (Decision call)
- Stop before tool-tour mode

**Step 5: Edit and upload**

Edit to remove friction, not to look fancy.

Upload, pin the CTA, and move on.

**Step 6: Reply handling (Daily)**

Long form is not post and disappear.

Same-day rule:
- Reply the same day
- Route serious interest to the decision call
- Do not consult in comments

**Step 7: Log and fix**

Log what created intent.

Next week you change one thing.

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Date | Publish date |
| Video title | Title |
| Hook line | The opening hook |
| CTA used | Link/action |
| Signal notes | Comments, DMs, clicks, booked calls |

## Short Form Kit (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts)

### When to Choose Short Form

Choose short form when you want daily reps and fast feedback.

Short form is the distribution layer. It turns one message into repeated contact with the market.

### Output Rules

- Short form: Post daily
- One message spine
- One CTA line
- You can post the same concept across platforms

You are not reinventing daily. You are repeating with small angle changes.

### Workflow

**Step 1: Research winners (5 to 7)**

Pick 5 to 7 winning shorts in your lane.

Save these from each:
- Hook style
- First few lines (the rhythm)
- The transition into the point
- The CTA style
- The comments (what triggered replies)

**Step 2: Angle change**

Same rule as long form.

Keep the flow, swap the language into your offer.

**Step 3: Short script (Simple and direct)**

Short scripts are not lectures. They are a pattern break plus one clear action.

Short spine:
- Hook (One line)
- Problem (One to two lines)
- Solution (A few lines, action-based)
- CTA (One line)

**Step 4: Film**

Film to ship.

Do not wait for perfect lighting or perfect takes.

**Step 5: Edit and upload**

Edit to cut dead space and keep the message clear.

Post, then move on.

**Step 6: Reply handling (Daily)**

Same-day rule applies:
- Reply the same day
- Move serious interest to the decision call
- Do not argue in comments
- Do not consult in DMs

**Step 7: Log and fix**

Log what created intent.

Next week you change one thing.

### Short Form Logging Fields

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Date | Publish date |
| Platform | IG / TikTok / Shorts |
| Hook line | The opening hook |
| Topic / angle | What the post is about |
| CTA used | Link/action |
| Signal notes | Comments, DMs, clicks |
| Next week | What you will keep or change |

## Text-Based Kit (LinkedIn, X, Threads)

### When to Choose Text

Choose text when you want speed, daily reps, and zero editing.

This is strong when your buyers live in text platforms and you want fast iteration.

### Output Rules

- Post daily
- One CTA only
- One core message repeated for the week
- Threads are allowed, but keep the flow simple

One post can be one point. One thread can be one point expanded.

### Workflow

**Step 1: Research winners (5 to 7)**

Pick 5 to 7 winning posts or threads in your lane.

Save these from each:
- Hook style
- Post structure (how it moves)
- The strongest line
- The CTA style
- The replies (what triggered debate or questions)

**Step 2: Angle change**

Swap into your offer language:
- Who it is for
- The leak
- The cost
- The fix
- The CTA

**Step 3: Write to publish**

Write like a person, not a textbook.

Text spine:
- Hook (One line)
- Problem (A few lines showing the loop)
- Solution (Action rules, not theory)
- CTA (One line)

Thread spine:
- Hook
- Problem loop
- Simple rules
- CTA

No extra "teaching."

You are trying to create signal, not applause.

**Step 4: Publish**

Post and stay available.

**Step 5: Reply handling (Daily)**

Your replies are part of the content game.
- Reply the same day
- Route serious interest to the decision call
- Do not start consulting in public threads

**Step 6: Log and fix**

Log what created intent.

Next week you change one thing.

### Text Logging Fields

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Date | Publish date |
| Platform | LinkedIn / X / Threads |
| Hook line | The opening hook |
| Post type | Post / thread |
| CTA used | Link/action |
| Signal notes | Replies, DMs, clicks |
| Next week | What you will keep or change |

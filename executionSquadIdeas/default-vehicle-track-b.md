## D) Layering Rule (How Track B Becomes The Real Offer)

**This is the most important section.**

You start with the skeleton:
- conversion path
- pipeline
- follow-up

Then you let market feedback tell you what to add.

### Step 1: List The Problems Clients Will Reveal

- "We don't get enough traffic."
- "We get traffic but it doesn't convert."
- "Leads come in but response time is slow."
- "We miss calls and lose prospects."
- "We get DMs but nobody follows up."
- "People book but don't show."
- "We quote people and they disappear."
- "We have past customers but no reactivation."
- "We can't track what's happening."
- "Everything relies on the owner."

### Step 2: Add The Layer That Matches The Constraint

**Only after the spine is installed.**

#### If Traffic Is The Constraint
- Google SEO
- content engine
- Google Ads
- Facebook Ads
- partnerships and referrals

#### If Conversion Is The Constraint
- landing page iteration
- offer positioning based on objections
- friction reduction
- chatbot only if it increases completion

#### If Speed-To-Lead Is The Constraint
- instant response
- routing rules
- team notifications
- missed-call text back

#### If Admin Load And Calls Are The Constraint
- AI receptionist
- call routing
- after-hours capture

#### If No-Shows Are The Constraint
- reminder sequences
- confirmation loops
- reschedule automation

#### If Quote Follow-Up Is The Constraint
- quote follow-up sequences
- objection reply templates
- decision deadline nudges

#### If Reactivation Is The Constraint
- past lead revival
- past customer campaigns
- referral capture loops

**Rule: one layer at a time, on decision day only. No daily reinvention.**
